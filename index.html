Overview

Instead of providing a detailed step by step explanation (because i'm lazy and truth be told not super fluent in this yet), here's a simplified summary of how the simulation works.

Parallelizations

  • A set number of cars (defined by n) are sent forward with the goal of navigating through traffic.
  • These cars are referred to as parallelizations, representing alternate scenarios or potential paths the car could take.

Selecting the Best Car

  • Among all the parallelizations, one car is expected to be superior due to the inherent randomness of the neural network.
  • Each car undergoes a unique mutation, and the best car is determined by the one that has progressed the furthest, measured by its Y value.
  • In other words, higher the y value the more "performant" the car is.

Saving the Best Car

  • When you identify a high-performing car, click the save button (ðŸ’¾) to preserve it as your best car.
  • All future iterations/parallelizations will use this saved car as a base.
  • Each new mutation will slightly adjust the neural network parameters of this base car.
  • Continue saving the top-performing car until you achieve one that completes the course.

Deleting the Best Car

  • I highly recommend that before starting your first run, you click the discard (ðŸš®) button to "wipe the slate clean". This ensures that there are no cars defined as the best yet.
  • This feature is best used in case you accidentally save a mutation that you would rather not have (maybe the mutation is boring, bugged, etc.).
  • Note that this doesn't need to be used before saving a new best car, as the save function overwrites whatever your last best car was.
